# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

UniPilot AI serves university students managing overlapping coursework, deadlines, class schedules, study time, focus habits, and academic progress. The primary user moves between a laptop and a phone, often under time pressure, and needs the next academic action to remain clear without learning a complicated system.

## Product Purpose

UniPilot AI brings six future modules into one coherent student productivity application: Assignment Management, AI Study Planning, Class Schedule, Focus Session, GPA Tracking, and Workload-Risk Warning. Success means students can understand what needs attention, plan realistic study time, complete focused work, and review academic progress without stitching together disconnected tools.

Workload-Risk Warning is a planning aid that explains workload signals. It is not a medical or psychological diagnosis.

## Positioning

UniPilot AI turns scattered academic obligations into a practical, understandable plan of action.

## Brand Personality

Calm, capable, and grounded. The product should feel structured enough to earn trust during a busy semester, warm enough to avoid institutional coldness, and direct enough that students can act without decoding the interface.

## Anti-references

UniPilot AI must not resemble a generic AI-SaaS dashboard. Avoid purple gradients, glassmorphism, oversized rounded cards, dark promotional sections, decorative AI sparkles, fake intelligence, hero-metric layouts, and identical icon-card grids. It must not present unfinished features as functional or turn academic risk into a medicalized alarm.

## Design Principles

1. **Action before decoration.** Every screen should make the next academic action easy to identify.
2. **One system, distinct workflows.** Shared components and navigation create continuity while each academic module retains task-specific structure.
3. **Honest intelligence.** AI suggestions remain reviewable drafts and never imply certainty or become active without confirmation.
4. **Calm under pressure.** Dense information is organized through hierarchy, spacing, and progressive disclosure rather than visual noise.
5. **State is part of the product.** Loading, empty, error, disabled, success, offline, and synchronization states are designed as deliberately as primary content.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

The responsive web application targets WCAG 2.2 AA. Keyboard focus must always be visible; appropriate touch targets must be at least 44 by 44 CSS pixels; meaning must never rely on color alone; semantic HTML and accessible names are required; text and layout must withstand zoom and narrow viewports; motion must respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.
